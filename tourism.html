MESSAGE FROM HALF INCH HIMALAYAS

Recording Breaking Tourist Flow In Kashmir In An Over Decade

The Dispatch

16.2 million people across the world visiting Kashmir in India. Government claims that it is for the first time the region has witnessed such large number of tourists visited the region.

Revocation of Article-370 on 5 August 2019, according to government, is one of that inspired people to visit the valley as "peace returned." Tourism industry of J&K also received a significant boost.

Pahalgam, Gulmarg, Sonmarg were most visited areas. Tulip garden and Dal Lake apart from two Mughal Gardens in Srinagar witnessed huge rush in the capital city.

Many tourists claimed to have visited Kashmir for the first time and expressed their happiness. They termed Kashmir as heaven on earth. Jammu and Kashmir Government also plans to open tourism in border areas.

Border areas like Gurez and Bangus have huge potential to attract visitors because of their beauty. Mr Fazal-ul-Haseeb Director Tourism, was not available for comments due to his busy schedule. Locals say they look forward to make Kashmir a better destination for tourists in Asia.
